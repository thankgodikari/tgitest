### SECTION 3: YOUR WEEKLY WORKFLOW (what to do each week, step by step)


**Every Monday morning (Week Start — 15 minutes):**
1. Open Tab 1. Go to your current week row.
2. Read the entire row left to right. Understand the week before starting.
3. Open Tab 2. Note which DSA problems are assigned this week.
4. Open Tab 4. Check if a new testing skill is introduced this week. If yes, add it to your plan.
5. Open Tab 9. Fill in Week number. Leave all checkboxes empty — you will check them off as the week progresses.


**Every Day (during study — 2 minutes):**
- After completing a task, add 1 GitHub commit minimum (even a README update counts).
- Log your hours in Toggl https://toggl.com/ (or a simple note).


**Every Saturday Evening (Week End Review — 30 minutes):**
1. Open Tab 9. Fill in the weekly row completely:
   - Hours Logged (check Toggl or your notes)
   - GitHub Commits count
   - Khan Academy math: did you get 100% on every unit test? (If No — you do not advance math next week, you redo the unit)
   - LeetCode problems count
   - Exercism problems count
   - Test coverage met? (Yes/No)
   - Blog post published? (check box TRUE)
   - Book reading done? (check box TRUE)
   - Exit criteria met? (check box TRUE/FALSE)
2. Go to Tab 1. Change the Status column for the current week:
   - ✅ Complete — if ALL exit criteria are met
   - ⚠️ Partial — if some exit criteria not met (must revisit during buffer weeks)
3. If Status is ⚠️ Partial — write what is incomplete in the Notes column. You will address this in W42–44 buffer.


**Every Sunday (Full Rest + Light Review — 2 hours max):**
- Write your weekly blog post (dev.to or Hashnode) — publish before midnight.
- Re-read your notes from the week — no new learning.
- Optionally: read from Tab 5 (book reading only).
- Do NOT code, watch lectures, or do LeetCode. Sunday is consolidation and rest.

### SECTION 7: HOW TO USE THE MATH TRACK


Math is studied in two ways simultaneously:


**Way 1 — Khan Academy (conceptual):**
Follow Tab 1's "Math Resource" column exactly. The exact unit and URL are listed. Complete 100% of the listed unit including the unit test. If the unit test score is below 100%, redo the exercises until you pass. Do not advance to next week's math until you score 100%.


**Way 2 — Math Projects (applied):**
After completing the Khan Academy unit, build the corresponding project from Tab 7. This cements the concept in code. Tab 1's "Math Project" column tells you which project. Tab 7 gives the full description, language, and what to implement.


**Math topics flow through the curriculum in this order:**
Binary Logic → Pre-Algebra → Algebra I → Algebra II → Logs/Exponents → Discrete Math (Logic, Proofs, Graphs, Combinatorics) → Pre-Calculus → Calculus (Limits, Derivatives, Integrals, Applied) → Linear Algebra (Vectors, Matrices, Eigenvalues/SVD) → Statistics + Probability (Stat 110) → Multivariable Calculus → Convex Optimization → Cryptography Math
